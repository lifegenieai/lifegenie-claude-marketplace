## Priority Skills for 2026

Based on LinkedIn's "Skills on the Rise 2025" report and cross-source analysis:

**Technical (high-demand):** AI Literacy, Large Language Models, Generative AI, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, Cloud Computing, SaaS, Digital Transformation

**Business (high-demand):** Product Development, Strategic Planning, Business Development, Entrepreneurship, Solution Selling, Go-to-Market Strategy

**Include both full terms and acronyms** ("Artificial Intelligence (AI)") for maximum search coverage.

**"Soft" skills** like "Emotional Intelligence" and "Strategic Leadership" are increasingly valued to differentiate from AI agents, but should be supported by recommendations rather than just listed.

## Keyword Placement Weights

Priority order from highest to lowest search weight:

1. **Headline** — highest visibility, follows you everywhere on platform
2. **Job Titles in Experience** — actually carry the highest search weight (even more than headline per some analyses)
3. **About / Summary** — tiebreaker function; serves conversion more than discovery
4. **Experience Descriptions** — indexed, contributes to semantic coherence
5. **Skills Section** — primary SEO keywords, 92% of recruiters use for filtering
6. **Services Section** — independently ranked in search
7. **Custom URL** — indexed by Google; contributes to external SEO
8. **Project Names** — highly weighted in search; often overlooked

Use 7-10 target keywords distributed naturally. Avoid stuffing — both LinkedIn and Google penalize unnatural density.
