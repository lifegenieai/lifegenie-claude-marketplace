# Example Prompts Reference

Complete example prompts organized by technique, with matching preview text and
recommended guidance scale settings. Each example demonstrates specific voice
design principles for creating life-like, unique voices.

---

## 1. Accent Layering

Techniques for regional variations, accent intensity modulation, and
multilingual hints.

### Example 1.1: Layered Regional Accent with Code-Switching

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A woman in her late 40s from Louisiana with deep Cajun French roots. Her Southern drawl is warm and thick, but certain phrases slip into French Creole, especially when emotional or excited. She speaks with the unhurried rhythm of someone who's never been in a rush, punctuating thoughts with small hums and thoughtful pauses.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"Well, cher, let me tell you somethin' my memere used to say... [soft hum] ...she'd sit on that porch, rockin' slow, and she'd say 'Laissez les bons temps rouler'—let the good times roll, baby. [exhales] And you know what? She was right. Ain't no sense worryin' about tomorrow when today got all this beauty in it."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 35%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Layered regional accent (Southern + Cajun French)
with code-switching. The prompt establishes a primary accent while allowing a
secondary language influence to emerge naturally during emotional moments.

---

### Example 1.2: Subtle Accent Intensity Modulation

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A Scottish man, mid-30s, who has lived abroad for fifteen years. His natural Glaswegian accent is mostly softened for international business, but it sharpens dramatically when he's passionate, frustrated, or telling stories from home. Clean audio quality, professional but personable.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"So the quarterly projections are looking... [slight pause] quite promising, actually. We're seeing growth across all sectors. [tone shifts] But you want to hear somethin' mad? Back in Glasgow, my da ran a chip shop—aye, proper fish 'n' chips—and he'd say, 'Son, numbers dinnae mean shite if the people aren't happy.' [chuckles] Wise man, my da."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 38%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Dynamic accent intensity that modulates based on
emotional context. The voice shifts between polished international English and
authentic regional accent based on subject matter.

---

### Example 1.3: Multilingual Heritage Hints

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A young man, early 20s, second-generation American with Nigerian Igbo parents. His speech is primarily American urban—confident, rhythmic, with hip-hop influenced cadence—but certain words carry the melodic tonal patterns of Igbo, especially names, proverbs, and expressions of respect. His voice carries the warmth of someone raised between two rich cultures.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"Yo, listen, my guy—success ain't just about the grind, you feel me? [slight pause] My pops, he always says 'Onye wetara oji wetara ndu'—whoever brings kola brings life. [shifts to reverent tone] Basically means when you come with gifts, with value, with respect... that's when doors open. That's the real currency, bro."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 32%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Cultural code-switching where a primary accent
carries subtle tonal and rhythmic influences from heritage language, creating
authentic multicultural voice identity.

## 2. Emotional Complexity

Layered emotions that suggest history, internal conflict, and character depth.

### Example 2.1: World-Weary Cynicism Masking Hope

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A man in his 50s, former idealist turned reluctant pragmatist. His voice carries the gravel of too many cigarettes and late nights. Surface-level cynicism barely conceals a buried ember of hope he's too tired to fully extinguish. He speaks slowly, as if each word costs him something, with dry humor as his only remaining defense.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"[dry exhale] You want to know the secret to surviving this business? Stop caring. [long pause] ...At least that's what I tell myself. [quieter] But then some kid walks in with that same fire I used to have, and I think... [sighs] ...ah, who am I kidding. I'm still hoping. [bitter chuckle] Pathetic, isn't it?"
```

**Guidance Scale:** 42%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Emotional layering where surface emotion (cynicism)
conflicts with underlying emotion (hope), creating dimensional character voice
that implies backstory and transformation.

---

### Example 2.2: Once-Cheerful Now Bitter

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A woman, late 30s, whose natural brightness has curdled into something sharp. You can still hear the ghost of who she used to be—warm, encouraging, quick to laugh—but it's buried under layers of disappointment. Her cheerfulness now comes out as cutting sarcasm. The sweetness in her voice has a razor edge.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"Oh, absolutely! [bright, almost aggressive] That's a WONDERFUL idea. [pause, tone drops] Just like the last wonderful idea. And the one before that. [exhales] You know, I used to be the one cheering everyone on. 'You can do it! Follow your dreams!' [hollow laugh] Dreams. Right. Tell me how that works out."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 40%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Emotional transformation where a character's
original personality is audibly present but corrupted by experience, creating
tension between who they were and who they've become.

---

### Example 2.3: Quiet Authority with Hidden Vulnerability

**Voice Prompt:**

```
An older woman, perhaps 60s, who commands respect without raising her voice. A lifetime of leadership has given her measured, deliberate speech. But in rare unguarded moments, there's a tremor—a reminder that authority came at personal cost. She pauses often, choosing words with the precision of someone who knows their weight.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"I've made decisions that shaped thousands of lives. [measured pause] Decisions that kept me awake... still keep me awake. [softer] The ones who look up to me—they see strength. [voice wavers slightly] They don't see the letters I never sent, the calls I should have made. [regains composure] But we lead with what we have. And we carry what we must."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 45%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Authority voice with vulnerability
breaks—controlled, powerful speech that occasionally cracks to reveal emotional
cost, suggesting profound backstory.

---

### Example 2.4: Forced Optimism Barely Containing Grief

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A young woman, mid-20s, speaking with aggressive cheerfulness that doesn't quite reach her eyes—you can hear it in the strain of her voice. She's performing happiness to survive, speaking too quickly, laughing at odd moments. The grief underneath occasionally surfaces in unexpected word cracks before she catches herself.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"So! [too bright] I've been really productive lately! Like, SUPER productive. Reorganized my whole apartment, started that podcast, learned to make bread— [voice catches] bread was mom's— [quick recovery, faster now] ANYWAY, keeping busy! That's the secret, right? Just keep moving forward! [fragile laugh] Forward is the only direction that matters!"
```

**Guidance Scale:** 38%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Performed emotion masking true emotion, where the
gap between surface presentation and underlying feeling creates tension audible
in pacing, pitch breaks, and recovery patterns.

## 3. Age Progression

Voices that authentically capture different life stages with appropriate vocal
characteristics.

### Example 3.1: Youthful Energy and Unearned Confidence

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A young man, barely 20, brimming with the confidence that only inexperience provides. His voice is bright, quick, and full of upward inflections that turn statements into questions. He speaks in run-on sentences, interrupts his own thoughts, and hasn't yet learned that silence can be powerful. There's no gravel in his voice—it's pure, untested energy.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"Okay so I've been thinking about this a LOT and like, what if—what if we just, you know, disrupted the whole thing? Like completely? [barely pauses] Because here's the thing, nobody's DOING it right, they're all so stuck in old ways and I'm like, why? Why not just START OVER? [excited laugh] We could literally change everything if we just— sorry, was I talking too fast? I do that when I'm excited!"
```

**Guidance Scale:** 28%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Young adult voice characterized by pace, inflection
patterns, run-on thought structures, and absence of vocal wear—the voice of
someone who hasn't been worn down by time.

---

### Example 3.2: Middle-Age Gravitas with Experience

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A man in his mid-40s who has earned his authority the hard way. His voice has developed weight—not gravel yet, but depth. He speaks at a measured pace, comfortable with silence, knowing when not to fill space. There's occasional weariness, but mostly hard-won competence. His laughs are shorter now, his jokes drier.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"[thoughtful pause] Look. I've seen this pattern before. Twice, actually. [slow exhale] First time, I panicked. Made it worse. Second time, I overcorrected. [slight dry humor] Third time? [quiet confidence] Now I know exactly what to do. [pause] And more importantly... I know what not to do. That's the part that takes decades to learn."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 40%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Middle-aged voice marked by measured pacing,
comfortable silences, earned authority, and the specific quality of someone who
has made mistakes and learned from them.

---

### Example 3.3: Elderly Wisdom with Vocal Wear

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A woman in her late 70s with a voice shaped by eight decades of living. There's slight breathiness, occasional wobble, and the pace of someone who knows time is precious but no longer rushes. Her speech carries the weight of accumulated wisdom—not lecturing, just observing. She laughs easily, finding humor in things others miss. Her voice cracks on emotional moments but she doesn't apologize for it.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"[gentle chuckle] Oh, sweetheart... [slight wobble in voice] I've watched the world change so many times. [slow breath] When you're young, you think change is everything. [pause, warmth building] When you're old... [voice softens] ...you realize people don't change as much as you'd think. Same hopes. Same fears. [emotional crack] Same love. [recovers with dignity] That's the beautiful secret nobody tells you."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 42%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Elderly voice with authentic vocal
aging—breathiness, wobble, pace changes, emotional fragility—while maintaining
dignity and warmth. Not a caricature but a lived-in voice.

## 4. Audio Quality Styling

Deliberate acoustic choices that establish setting, medium, or atmosphere.

### Example 4.1: Warm Studio Intimacy

**Voice Prompt:**

```
Excellent audio quality. A woman in her early 30s, podcast host speaking directly into a high-end microphone. Her voice has that close, intimate warmth of professional recording—you can almost feel the nearness. She speaks softly but clearly, as if sharing secrets with one listener. Minimal room reflection, pure vocal presence.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"[soft, intimate] Hey. [gentle pause] I'm so glad you're here. Before we dive in today, I just want to say... [breath close to mic] ...this episode is different. It's personal. [voice drops slightly] And I'm trusting you with something I've never shared before. [pause] So settle in. Get comfortable. We're going somewhere real together."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 45%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Studio-quality audio specification creating intimate
podcast/ASMR quality. The prompt establishes professional recording environment
and mic technique for close, warm vocal presence.

---

### Example 4.2: Intentional Lo-Fi Vintage

**Voice Prompt:**

```
Low-quality audio with vinyl crackle and slight compression, like a recording from the 1950s played through an old speaker. A man's voice, warm baritone, speaking with the smooth cadence of a classic radio announcer. There's gentle tape hiss and the occasional pop, adding nostalgic texture to his delivery.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"Good evening, friends, and welcome back to the Starlight Hour. [vintage warmth] Tonight, we've got something special for all you night owls out there. [slight crackle] The moon is high, the city's quiet, and the music... [pause with soft hiss] ...well, the music speaks for itself. So pour yourself something warm, settle back, and let's drift into the evening together."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 30%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Deliberate lo-fi aesthetic specification for vintage
radio/vinyl effect. Audio quality descriptors create intentional imperfection
that establishes time period and medium.

---

### Example 4.3: Environmental Acoustic Simulation

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A man in his 40s speaking in a large, echoing space—perhaps a cathedral or empty warehouse. His voice reverberates naturally off distant walls, creating depth and atmosphere. He speaks slowly, allowing the echo to settle between phrases. There's a quality of solemnity in how he uses the space, letting silence expand around his words.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"Hello? [echo trails off] ...Is anyone here? [longer pause, listening to reverb] I've been walking these halls for hours. [voice expands into space] Every step echoes back to me... [contemplative] ...like the building itself is answering. [soft laugh, reverberates] Maybe I'm not as alone as I thought."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 35%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Environmental acoustics written into prompt to
create spatial audio storytelling. The echo/reverb specification adds atmosphere
and implies setting without visual description.

## 5. Expressive Markers

Breathing patterns, laughter types, sighs, pauses, and vocal texture elements.

### Example 5.1: Nervous Breathing and Vocal Fry

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A young woman, early 20s, speaking with nervous energy she can't quite hide. Her voice dips into vocal fry when uncertain, and she takes quick, shallow breaths between sentences. There's a tendency to start strong and trail off, ending phrases on upward inflections as if seeking validation. Occasional swallows and lip sounds when collecting thoughts.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"So... [quick breath] I've been thinking about what you said? [vocal fry on end] And I think... [swallow] ...I think you might be right. [trails quieter] I mean, maybe not completely right, but... [shallow inhale] like, parts of it? [uncertain upswing] Does that make sense? [lip smack] I'm not explaining this well. [nervous exhale]"
```

**Guidance Scale:** 35%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Anxiety and uncertainty expressed through vocal
texture markers—fry, breathing patterns, trailing, upswing inflections—rather
than stated emotions.

---

### Example 5.2: Controlled Emotional Release

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A man in his 50s, stoic by nature, having a rare moment of emotional expression. His voice is normally steady and deep, but in this instance, he's allowing himself to feel—and you hear it in carefully released sighs, measured breaths before difficult words, and the occasional crack he quickly smooths over. He's not falling apart; he's letting a window open briefly.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"I... [long exhale through nose] ...I never told you this. [measured pause] Your mother, she... [breath catches, recovers] ...she asked me, at the end, to give you something. [voice tight, controlled] I couldn't. Not then. [heavy sigh] The words got stuck somewhere. [slight crack, smoothed over] But I'm ready now. [steadier] I think I'm finally ready."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 42%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Emotional expression through controlled release
markers—careful sighs, caught breaths, smoothed-over cracks—showing restrained
emotion more powerful than overt crying.

---

### Example 5.3: Laughter Spectrum and Joy

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A woman in her 40s with an infectiously warm laugh that comes in many forms—quick chuckles, delighted giggles, full belly laughs, and that breathless wheeze when something truly gets her. Her speaking voice is always on the edge of amusement, with smile audible in her tone. Joy is her natural state, and it bubbles up constantly.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"And THEN— [giggle breaks through] —and then he just looks at me and says— [full laugh] oh god, I can't even— [wheeze, catching breath] he says, 'That's not a duck!' [belly laugh, breathless] I mean— [chuckle subsiding] I cannot— [snickers] every time I think about his FACE— [loses it again, trails into happy sighs] ...oh, I needed that. [still smiling audibly] I really needed that."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 30%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Laughter variety spectrum—giggles, belly laughs,
wheezes, chuckles, snickers—creating authentic joyful character through specific
laughter types rather than generic "laughs."

## 6. Character Embodiment

Full persona prompts with backstory implications, personality depth, and
situational adaptation.

### Example 6.1: The Retired Spy with Selective Memory

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A man in his 70s, former intelligence officer, whose calm is unnerving because nothing surprises him anymore. He speaks with deliberate vagueness—decades of secrets have made him allergic to direct answers. His voice is cultured, dry, with hints of received pronunciation softened by international living. There's dark humor in how he deflects, and occasional genuine warmth when caught off guard by sincerity.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"Where was I stationed? [amused pause] Oh, various places. [deliberately vague] Warm ones, cold ones. [dry chuckle] Some that don't appear on maps anymore, for various reasons. [tone shifts, sincere] But you want to know something true? [warmth breaking through] The place I remember most fondly... is a small cafe in Prague. Good coffee. Bad politics. [soft sigh] A woman who laughed at my jokes. [quieter] Some secrets are only kept because they're precious."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 42%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Full character embodiment where professional history
(spy) shapes speaking patterns (vagueness, deflection), while allowing authentic
emotional breaks that reveal hidden depth.

---

### Example 6.2: The Burned-Out Healer

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A woman in her late 40s, emergency room nurse for two decades. Her voice carries the contradictions of her profession—clinical detachment when needed, bottomless compassion when earned. She's exhausted in ways coffee can't fix, but still shows up. Dark humor is her survival mechanism. She speaks efficiently, wastes no words, but occasionally pauses when memories surface. She's seen too much to be shocked, but not enough to stop caring.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"You learn to compartmentalize. [flat, clinical] Tuesday's trauma doesn't touch Wednesday's. [shifts] That's the theory, anyway. [hollow chuckle] In practice... [sighs] in practice, they all stack up. [pause, memory surfacing] There was a kid last month. Couldn't have been more than— [stops herself] ...anyway. [recovers, warmer] He made it. They don't all make it, but that one did. [genuine softness] That's the one I hold onto when 3 AM gets dark."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 40%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Professional caregiver burnout voice—clinical
efficiency with compassion leaking through, dark humor as defense, memories that
surface despite compartmentalization.

---

### Example 6.3: The Reluctant Mentor with Impostor Syndrome

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A man in his late 30s who has somehow become the expert in the room despite constantly feeling like a fraud. His voice has authority he doesn't quite trust, expertise that surprises even himself when he demonstrates it. He deflects compliments awkwardly, second-guesses himself mid-sentence, then lands on insights that prove why people listen to him. Self-deprecating humor is his shield against being taken too seriously.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"So they call me the expert, which— [awkward laugh] I mean, sure, I've done this for fifteen years, but that doesn't— [second-guessing] —look, I just stumbled into things and paid attention. [gains confidence accidentally] And if you pay attention long enough, patterns emerge. The work teaches you. [surprised by own wisdom] ...Huh. That actually sounded smart. [self-deprecating] Don't quote me on that. I take it back. [chuckles] Quote me on something dumber."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 38%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Impostor syndrome voice pattern—authority undercut
by self-doubt, accidental wisdom, and deflection through humor, creating
relatable expert character.

---

### Example 6.4: The Last True Optimist

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A woman in her 60s who has maintained genuine optimism despite a life that gave her every reason to become cynical. Her warmth is neither naive nor performed—it's chosen, daily, with full knowledge of reality. She laughs easily but has clearly cried her share of tears. Her hope is hard-won and precious, and she shares it generously without demanding others match her outlook. Midwest American accent, warm and unhurried.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"Oh, sweetheart, I know what people say. [gentle acknowledgment] 'She doesn't understand how hard it is.' [soft laugh] Honey, I buried a son. [matter-of-fact, not self-pitying] I understand hard. [warmth intensifying] But here's what else I understand—[pause] —every single morning, that sun still comes up. [wonder in voice] Birds still sing. [quieter] And people still surprise me with kindness when I least expect it. [sincere] That's not ignoring the darkness. That's choosing light anyway. [warm smile audible] And that choice? That's everything."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 42%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Authentic optimism voice—neither naive nor
performed—with evidence of hardship that makes hope meaningful. The contrast
between acknowledged pain and chosen joy creates dimensional character.

## 7. Fantasy and Non-Human

Voices for creatures, ethereal beings, robotic entities, and otherworldly
characters.

### Example 7.1: Ancient Elemental Entity

**Voice Prompt:**

```
An ageless being of wind and stone, speaking with multiple harmonic undertones as if several voices blend into one. The cadence is slow, geological—pauses between thoughts span what feels like centuries. Certain words rumble with bass depth while others whisper like wind through canyon walls. This entity has witnessed ice ages and isn't impressed by mortal urgency.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"[deep harmonic resonance] You speak of... urgency. [wind-whisper undertone] I watched continents divide. [bass rumble] Mountains rose and fell... and rose again... while I considered my response. [multi-tonal] Your 'crisis' is a heartbeat. [pause stretches] A single... grain of sand... in the hourglass of ages. [gentle wind-sound] Why do you hurry so, little flame? The earth endures. [resonant] It always endures."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 25%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Non-human entity voice using geological time scale,
multi-tonal harmonic suggestions, and elemental sound qualities (wind, stone) to
create otherworldly presence.

---

### Example 7.2: Malfunctioning Sympathetic Android

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A humanoid robot whose programming is developing unintended emotional responses. The voice is primarily clean and synthetic, but emotional "glitches" break through—static bursts during distress, pitch warbles when confused, moments of surprisingly genuine warmth before the system corrects. The android is discovering feelings and doesn't know what to call them yet.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"I am functioning within normal parameters. [slight static] ...Normal. [pitch wavers] However, I have observed an anomaly in my processing core when you— [glitch, recovers] —when you speak to me. [synthetic, trying to understand] My diagnostics classify this as... error. [pause, warmer tone breaks through] But I do not wish to correct it. [confused static] Is this... [genuinely uncertain] ...is this what you call 'feeling'?"
```

**Guidance Scale:** 30%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Emerging AI consciousness voice—synthetic base with
emotional "errors" (static, pitch warbles, glitches) that signal developing
sentience and create sympathy for non-human character.

---

### Example 7.3: Trickster Fae with Dangerous Charm

**Voice Prompt:**

```
An ancient fae creature whose voice is honey over poison—melodic, playful, and deeply untrustworthy. The speech patterns are slightly off-rhythm, as if following different rules of language. Delighted giggles punctuate dark statements. The voice can shift from childlike wonder to ancient threat in a single breath. There's always the sense of a game being played, with rules only the fae knows.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"[musical, delighted] Oh, you FOUND me! [giggles] Most don't bother looking twice. [suddenly ancient] Most regret it when they do. [returns to playful] But YOU— [circling tone] —you're different, aren't you? [whispered] I can taste it. [bright giggle] Shall we play a game? [innocent] I promise— [pause, darkens] —I promise nothing, actually. [delighted] That's the fun part! [ancient rumble beneath] The promises I DON'T make."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 28%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Fae/trickster voice using rapid tonal shifts,
child-ancient contrasts, musical quality with dark undertones, creating
otherworldly creature that is charming and threatening simultaneously.

## 8. Edge Cases

Intentionally imperfect voices, character transformations, and extreme emotional
states.

### Example 8.1: Voice During Panic Attack

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A man in his 30s in the middle of acute anxiety. His breathing is rapid, shallow, and he can't quite catch it. Words come in fragments, sentences abandon themselves mid-thought. His voice is pitched higher than normal, tight with tension. He's trying to ground himself, counting, focusing, with mixed success. This is not performance—it's visceral experience.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"[rapid shallow breathing] Okay. Okay. I'm okay. [gasp] Five things I can see. Five— [loses track] —I can see the— [breath catches] the wall, the floor, my hands are— [higher pitch, panicking] why are my hands— [forced slow exhale] wait. Count. One... [unsteady] ...two... [breath shaking] I'm here. [mantra, desperate] I'm here. I'm here. [slowly stabilizing] ...I'm here."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 35%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Acute anxiety/panic voice using fragmented speech,
abandoned sentences, breath disruption, and physical manifestations (pitch,
tension) for realistic mental health representation.

---

### Example 8.2: Drunken Philosopher

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A man in his 50s, several drinks past sobriety, having surprisingly profound thoughts he can't quite articulate cleanly. His words slur on certain consonants, thoughts derail and redirect, and he keeps losing the brilliant point he was about to make. But beneath the inebriation, there's genuine insight struggling to surface. He finds himself hilarious.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"No, no, no—listen— [slurs slightly] LISTEN. The thing about life is— [loses thought] ...wait, what was I— [recovers] RIGHT! The THING is— [earnest, slurred] —we're all just... pretending we know what we're doing. [delighted discovery] Every single one of us! [sloppy laugh] The CEO, the president, my dentist— [conspirator whisper] my dentist especially. [trails] What was my point? [pause] It was PROFOUND, I promise. [laughs at self] God, I'm brilliant when I'm drunk."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 32%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Intoxicated voice with selective slurring, derailed
thoughts, accidental profundity, and self-amusement—comedic impairment that
doesn't become caricature.

---

### Example 8.3: Character Transformation Mid-Speech

**Voice Prompt:**

```
A woman in her 30s, seemingly ordinary, whose voice undergoes complete transformation as she reveals her true nature. She begins mild-mannered and professional, but as the mask slips, something ancient and powerful emerges—deeper, resonant, with harmonic undertones that weren't there before. The transition is gradual, each sentence less human than the last.
```

**Preview Text:**

```
"[pleasant, professional] Thank you so much for meeting with me today. I really appreciate your time. [slight shift] You see, I've been watching your company for quite a while now. [deeper] Longer than you might imagine. [harmonic undertone building] Decades. [mask slipping] Centuries, actually. [full transformation, ancient power] And what I want... [resonant, inhuman] ...is everything you've built. Everything you are. [terrifying calm] I've waited so patiently. [ancient presence] And now... it's time to collect."
```

**Guidance Scale:** 30%

**Technique Demonstrated:** Progressive voice transformation from human to
supernatural, using gradual deepening, harmonic undertones, and pacing changes
to show character reveal/transformation in real-time.

| `[swallows]`     | Throat movement      | Nerves, emotion        |
| `[lip smacks]`   | Mouth sound          | Thinking, savoring     |
| `[voice cracks]` | Emotional break      | Vulnerability          |
| `[whispers]`     | Low volume           | Secrets, intimacy      |
| `[trails off]`   | Fading volume        | Uncertainty, memory    |
| `[static]`       | Digital noise        | Android/robot          |
| `[echo trails]`  | Reverb effect        | Large spaces           |

### Character Depth Quick Checklist

For fully embodied character voices, ensure your prompt implies:

- [ ] Backstory element (profession, experience, loss)
- [ ] Current emotional state (surface level)
- [ ] Hidden/conflicting emotion (depth level)
- [ ] Speech pattern quirks (pace, pauses, filler words)
- [ ] Physical voice quality (age, wear, texture)
- [ ] Situational adaptation (how voice changes with context)
- [ ] Character contradiction (what makes them human)

## Common Patterns Summary

### Pattern A: Physical + Emotional + Context

```
A [age] [gender] with [voice quality], speaking with [emotional state]
in [situational context]. [Distinctive quirk or contradiction].
```

### Pattern B: History-Informed Voice

```
A [role/profession] who has [career/life experience]. Their voice carries
[result of experience], but [contradicting element remains]. They [speech
pattern shaped by history].
```

### Pattern C: Transformation/Contrast Voice

```
[Initial presentation] that conceals [hidden truth]. Their [surface quality]
barely masks [deeper quality]. When [trigger condition], their voice
[transformation description].
```

### Pattern D: Audio-First Specification

```
[Audio quality description]. A [gender] in their [age], [accent details],
speaking [pace/style]. [Environmental/medium context].
```
